Lesson 0007 · D&D 5e

Spellcasting

The resource system, how spells resolve, and what the DM bot must track the moment a wizard joins the party.

Combat got manageable once you understood the action economy. Spellcasting adds a second resource layer on top of it: spell slots. A spell slot is consumed to cast a spell of that level or lower. Run out of slots and the caster is left with cantrips. This lesson covers everything the bot needs to adjudicate a spell — from the moment the player announces it to the moment it hits.

D&D 5e The Two Resources

Cantrips — unlimited

Cantrips are 0th-level spells. They cost no spell slot, can be cast any number of times, and never run out. Fire Bolt, Minor Illusion, Prestidigitation are all cantrips. The bot never needs to track cantrip usage — they're always available.

Spell Slots — limited

Leveled spells (1st through 9th level) consume a spell slot of at least that level. Slots do not recharge during a short rest for most classes — they come back on a long rest. The bot must track current slots per level for each caster.

Spell level ≠ character level. A 5th-level wizard has access to 3rd-level spell slots. A 1st-level spell like Magic Missile can be cast using a 3rd-level slot for extra effect. The slot level is the resource; the spell level is the minimum slot required.

D&D 5e How Spells Resolve

Every spell uses one of two resolution paths — either the caster attacks or the target saves. The DM never gets to choose: it's specified in the spell's description.

Spell Attack Roll
Caster rolls d20 + spellcasting modifier + proficiency vs target AC. Works exactly like a weapon attack roll — critical hits apply (double damage dice on a natural 20).
d20 + spell mod + prof vs AC
Examples: Fire Bolt, Eldritch Blast, Chromatic Orb
Saving Throw
Target rolls d20 + their save modifier vs the caster's Spell Save DC. On a success, the target usually takes half damage or suffers no effect (spell specifies which).
target: d20 + save mod vs DC
DC = 8 + prof + spell mod
Examples: Fireball (DEX save), Hold Person (WIS save)

The Spellcasting Modifier

Each class uses a different ability score for spellcasting — this affects both spell attack rolls and Spell Save DC:

Upcasting

A spell cast using a higher-level slot than its minimum is upcast. Most spells gain a bonus — typically more damage dice. The spell description always says what changes. Magic Missile at 1st level fires 3 darts; at 3rd level it fires 5. Fireball deals 8d6 at 3rd level and gains 1d6 per slot level above that.

What the bot must track per spellcaster:
spellcasting ability score  ·  proficiency bonus  ·  current slots per level [1–5]  ·  max slots per level  ·  concentration (currently active spell or nil)

D&D 5e Concentration

Many powerful spells require the caster to maintain concentration — an ongoing mental effort that can be broken. You can only concentrate on one spell at a time. Casting a second concentration spell ends the first automatically, with no save.

What breaks concentration
✗ Casting another concentration spell — first spell ends immediately
✗ Taking damage — CON saving throw (DC = 10 or half damage taken, whichever is higher)
✗ Being incapacitated or killed — automatically broken
✓ Taking other actions, moving, attacking — concentration is maintained
✓ Casting non-concentration spells — fine, concentration continues

The Concentration Save

DC = max(10, damage_taken / 2)
Roll: d20 + CON modifier ≥ DC → concentration holds

Example: a wizard concentrating on Hold Person takes 18 damage from a sword strike. The DC is max(10, 9) = 10. They roll d20 + CON modifier. Fail, and Hold Person ends immediately — the frozen target is freed.

BG3 note: every time an enemy attacks a concentrating character and you see a "Concentration Check" roll — that's this. The game runs it automatically. Your bot has to trigger it explicitly whenever a concentrating PC takes damage.

D&D 5e The Bonus Action Spell Rule

This rule surprises almost everyone the first time. If you cast any spell as a bonus action on your turn, the only spell you can cast as your action is a cantrip — not a leveled spell, even if you have the slots.

Bonus action spell?What you can cast as your action
NoAny spell (leveled or cantrip)
Yes (e.g. Healing Word)Cantrips only

The rationale: bonus action spells are fast (healing a downed ally, applying a buff). Letting casters pair them with a full action spell would be too powerful. The bot must enforce this constraint in the action validation layer.
